Introduction

Dufu(712-770CE) is one of the China’s greatest poets during the Tang Dynasty. He is a poet-historian who conveyed the emotional impact, political impact and social issues for the whole society. Based on the displaced life experience in his early age, he wrote poems about the hardship and the traveling experience.

Representative works

Pounding the Clothes

You won’t return from the front.

I clean the laundry stone in autumn.

The bitter cold months are near;

My heart aches with long separation.

Can I shirk the toil of pounding your clothes?

No, they must go to the Great Wall.

Let me use all my woman’s strength.

May you, my lord, hear the sound o’er the vast.

Farewell to my Soldier-Friend

The Jo River flows to the land’s end;

The Yang Pass looms on the horizon.

Now you will cross the sand-bar;

There smoke didn’t rise for months.

To show your valor, take no care for life;

To be a marquis, do not count the years.

In winter, keep your horse from being lost,

Or snow will bury his broidered saddle.
